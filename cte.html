The mission of Career and Technical Education is to help students discover educational purpose, personal potential and a pathway to their future.

  • CTE provides students with technical training to prepare for a successful career.
    • CTE is an essential component of the total educational system in Utah and is critical to the country’s ability to compete in a global economy.
    • CTE provides courses and pathways consistent with industry standards.
    • CTE areas of study include: Agriculture, Business, Economics and Entrepreneurship, Family and Consumer Sciences, Health Science, Information Technology, Marketing, Skilled and Technical Sciences, Technology and Engineering
    • CTE mission, goals, and guiding principles
  • The Goals of Career & Technical Education in Granite School District
    • Improve instructional quality through continuous professional development
    • Increase enrollment across all student populations, including non-traditional programs
    • Increase student achievement as measured by state skills testing
    • Increase student achievement levels in literacy, mathematics and science
    • Identify clear pathways for all CTE programs
    • Increase student graduation rates (9-12)
    • Increase student enrollment in post secondary training and job placement
    • Increase student leadership through participation in CTSOs
    • The Guiding Principles of Career and Technical Education in Granite Schools District
  • Relationships…
    • Students come first and are the reason for our existence.
    • If teachers are taken care of, they will take care of the students.
    • TEAMWORK – everyone belongs, everyone contributes. CTE is a family.
  • Rigor…
    • CTE courses must be aligned with other disciplines to promote real-world application of academic theory.
    • High expectations are critical in all CTE programs, along with a plan for when they are not achieved.
  • Relevance…
    • CTE programs are about change, quality and constant improvement.
    • Learning should be fun and meaningful.
    • All education is “career education”.
    • Clear pathways to further training and/or employment are important elements of all CTE courses and programs.
